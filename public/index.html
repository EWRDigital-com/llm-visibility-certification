What is LLM Visibility?

LLM visibility is how reliably AI answer engines find your brand, understand who you are, and cite you in their answers. It is the AI-era replacement for "do we rank?" — the new question is "do we get recalled and credited when a model answers?" The score above measures the on-page foundations of that; the biggest levers are off-domain (your third-party mention footprint) and are shown as a roadmap in every report.

The four pillars

Search Mentions

How broadly you're referenced across third-party sources, not just your own site. If no one beyond your domain talks about you, a model has little to work with.

LLM Surfacing

How often and how early you appear in AI answers to category questions, not just when prompted by name.

Authority Trust

Whether models treat you as safe to repeat — built on third-party reviews, recognizable citations, and confirmed authorship.

Citations Consistency

Whether a model can resolve your identity and attribute content to you. Mismatched names, bios, and profiles cause hallucination or silence.

The maturity ladder

Brands climb five rungs. The goal isn't to fix everything at once — it's to find the weakest pillar holding you at your current rung and unlock the next. On-page audits ceiling at Recognized; the top three rungs are earned off-domain.

  1. Invisible — models don't surface you at all.
  2. Recognized — your entity is known, but you're rarely cited.
  3. Referenced — you appear in answers without being named explicitly. The first real breakthrough.
  4. Authoritative — the shift from "mentioned" to "safe to recommend."
  5. Default Source — category ownership; you're reached for first.

How the score works

The LLM Visibility Score™ is a deterministic audit — same page in, same score out. It reads one URL and scores the on-page foundations of AI citation across three pillars (Foundation, Validation, Ingestion), checks whether AI crawlers can retrieve the page at all, and surfaces your weakest pillar as the bottleneck to fix first. The off-domain pillars — your third-party mention footprint and live answer presence — are the bigger levers, shown as a roadmap rather than scored from a single page, because they can't be honestly measured from one URL.
