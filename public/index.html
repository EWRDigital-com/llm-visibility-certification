What Is LLM Visibility?

LLM visibility is how reliably AI answer engines like ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, Gemini, and Claude find your brand, understand who you are, and cite you in their answers. It is the AI-era replacement for the question "do we rank?" — the new question is "do we get recalled and credited when a model answers?"

Why it is different from SEO

Classic SEO is about eligibility: being crawlable, indexable, and ranked. LLM visibility is about selection: whether a model chooses to repeat you when it composes an answer. A page can rank on page one and still never be cited in the AI answer sitting above it. The two are related — most AI engines pull from the organic results — but they are scored differently, and the levers that win citation are not the same ones that win a blue link.

The four pillars

LLM visibility breaks into four measurable pillars. Together they answer whether you are present, understood, trusted, and credited.

Search Mentions

How broadly you are referenced across third-party sources, not just your own site. If no one beyond your domain talks about you, a model has little to work with.

LLM Surfacing

How often and how early you appear in AI answers to category questions ("best CRM for small teams"), not just when prompted by name.

Authority Trust

Whether models treat you as safe to repeat — built on third-party reviews, recognizable citations, and confirmed authorship.

Citations Consistency

Whether a model can resolve your identity and attribute content to you. Mismatched names, bios, and profiles cause hallucination, misattribution, or silence.

The maturity ladder

Brands climb five rungs. The goal is not to fix everything at once — it is to find the weakest pillar holding you at your current rung and unlock the next one.

  1. Invisible — models do not surface you at all.
  2. Recognized — your entity is known, but you are rarely cited.
  3. Referenced — you appear in answers without being named explicitly. The first real breakthrough.
  4. Authoritative — the shift from "mentioned" to "safe to recommend."
  5. Default Source — category ownership; you are reached for first.

How the LLM Visibility Score measures it

The LLM Visibility Score™ is a deterministic audit. It reads a page and scores the on-page foundations of AI citation: structured data and entity identity, on-page citations and statistics, answer-formatted content, freshness, and crawler access for AI answer engines. It surfaces your weakest pillar (the bottleneck) and places you on the maturity ladder. The off-domain pillars — your third-party mention footprint and live answer presence — are the bigger levers, and they are shown as a roadmap rather than scored from a single page, because they cannot be honestly measured from one URL.
